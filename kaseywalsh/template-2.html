The Origin Story

One diagnosis.
A decade
of action.

You're not a scientist by training. You have a BA in Communications from Assumption University, a career that spanned conference coordination and healthcare services, and a life that changed irrevocably the day your daughter was diagnosed with AP-4-HSP (SPG47) — an ultra-rare neurodegenerative disorder affecting fewer than 250 families globally.

What followed was extraordinary. With no background in molecular biology, you taught yourself the science. You reached out to researchers. You co-founded a nonprofit. You built bridges between labs and living rooms — connecting families in Texas with scientists in Sheffield, and caregivers in Boston with researchers at Cambridge.

Your contributions directly supported biomarker discovery efforts, expanded recognition of hereditary spastic paraplegia as a misclassified patient population, and identified a research opportunity that led to a collaboration where computational drug discovery predicted three FDA-approved medications for potential off-label use in AP-4-HSP.

Today, you lead Winsights.life — a venture philanthropy that uses AI and machine learning to transform patient and caregiver lived experience into regulatory-grade real-world evidence. The mission: ensure that the data families generate every single day stops being invisible to the science that needs it most.
