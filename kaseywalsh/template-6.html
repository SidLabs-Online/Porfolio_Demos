About You

A communicator who became
a catalyst.

You grew up in a world of words — studying Communication and Media Studies at Assumption University, learning how to tell stories that move people.

You didn't know yet that the story you'd need to tell most urgently would be your own. Or that it would require you to learn molecular biology, build a global nonprofit, collaborate with researchers at four of the world's most prestigious institutions, and ultimately found a company.

What follows is your journey — not as a resume, but as a life lived fully in service of the people who needed you most.

Your Story

The Road to Here
and beyond

2002 – 2006

Healthcare Service Coordinator, Muscular Dystrophy Association

In-person patient liaison at Boston Children's Hospital, Mass General, and Lahey Clinic. Supported over 600 individuals with neuromuscular diseases. Organized parent and patient support groups. Attended summer camp for 80+ children. Your calling was already clear.

Career Begins

2006 – 2007

Speaker Coordinator, Pulvermedia (VON Conferences)

Curated high-impact speaker lineups for Jeff Pulver's groundbreaking Voice on the Net conferences — working during a period of intense technological disruption in IP communications. An early lesson in how conferences build ecosystems and catalyze movements.

Voice of Innovation

2007 – 2008

Education Coordinator, Straumann USA

Coordinated continuing education courses for dental professionals in implant and regenerative dentistry. Managed speakers, logistics, and cross-functional collaboration. Learned how structured learning experiences drive adoption, confidence, and clinical success.

Education & Events

Circa 2015–2016

The Diagnosis That Changed Everything

Robbie — your daughter, "super sweet, super sassy" — is diagnosed with AP-4-HSP (SPG47), a rare neurodegenerative disorder affecting fewer than 250 families globally. There is no treatment. Barely any research. No roadmap. You become the scientist, connector, and catalyst the community needs.

Life Turning Point

2016

Co-Founded Cure AP-4, Inc.

A pledge of hope between two families becomes a globally recognized nonprofit. Cure AP-4 launches to accelerate research and improve quality of life for children with AP-4-HSP across 4 subtypes: SPG47, SPG50, SPG51, and SPG52.

Nonprofit Founded

2016 – 2025

9 Years of Scientific Collaboration

Boston Children's Hospital. The Broad Institute. Sheffield University. Cambridge University. Unravel Biosciences. Pleozyme Inc. You build research partnerships across continents — contributing to biomarker discoveries, disease reclassification, and the prediction of 3 FDA-approved medications for AP-4-HSP off-label use.

Science & Partnership

c. 2022

Newburyport Inclusive Playground

Co-founded Newburyport's first playground designed for children of all abilities. The grassroots effort doesn't just build a playground — it triggers a city-wide policy commitment that every future park project will prioritize inclusion.

Community Impact

April 2024

Lindamae Lucas Award for Excellence

The Commonwealth of Massachusetts honors Kasey for embodying compassion, resilience, advocacy, and humor in the face of life's challenges — particularly in special needs parenting and mental health support.

State Recognition

March 2025

Founder & CEO, Winsights.life

You launch your most ambitious chapter: a mission-driven, AI-powered venture philanthropy to transform lived experience into regulatory-grade real-world evidence. Winsights.life is the bridge between patients, caregivers, clinicians, and researchers that you spent a decade wishing existed.
