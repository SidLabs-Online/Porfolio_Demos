The Person Behind the Research

A Communicator Who Became a Catalyst

You studied Communication and Media Studies at Assumption University. You began your career coordinating healthcare services for patients with neuromuscular diseases at the Muscular Dystrophy Association — sitting at the bedside of patients at Boston Children's Hospital, Mass General, and Lahey Clinic.

When your daughter Robbie was diagnosed with AP-4-HSP (SPG47), you combined every skill you had — communication, relationship-building, community organizing — and pointed them at a disease that had no treatment and almost no research funding.

You didn't publish papers. You built the ecosystem that makes papers possible. You served as a critical liaison between scientific teams and patient families, translating research findings into plain language across dozens of languages, and organized annual research summits for the Cure AP-4 community.

Today your work continues through Winsights.life — building the infrastructure for patient-generated data to finally enter the research pipeline where it can do the most good.
